Cookies and how they Benefit You

Our website uses cookies, as almost all websites do, to help provide you with the best experience we can. Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile phone when you browse websites.

Our cookies help us:

  • Make our website work as you’d expect
  • Remember your settings during and between visits
  • Improve the speed/security of the site
  • Allow you to share pages with social networks like Facebook
  • Continuously improve our website for you
  • Make our marketing more efficient (ultimately helping us to offer the service we do at the price we do)

We do not use cookies to:

  • Collect any personally identifiable information (without your express permission)
  • Collect any sensitive information (without your express permission)
  • Pass personally identifiable data to third parties

Science school trips to Florida

Orlando, Florida is the perfect destination for students of physics and biology, combining exciting excursions that link to the science KS3 and KS4 curriculum with a great destination for groups wanting to have both an educational trip that links to the curriculum combined with memorable visits to some of Orlando’s greatest theme parks. Science sStudents will be amazed by the Kennedy Space Centre where they will embark on a mission of a lifetime with a shuttle launch experience! A Boggy Creek Airboat Ride gives the opportunity to experience the wetlands and wildlife and your group may even spot the Florida alligator in its natural habitat. Choose from theme parks such as Universal Studios, Islands of Adventure Orlando, Disney’s Magic Kingdom or the Epcot Centre which offers many study opportunities for students including the history of the universe. A science trip to Florida offers a variety of excursions that will enthuse students upon returning to the classroom.

Suggested Itinerary

Orlando – GCSE Science, Additional Science, Chemistry, Biology and Physics

Aimed at GCSE science students, this itinerary has been developed in partnership with Equity’s science consultant, an experienced teacher of the subject.

  • DETAILED
  • BASIC

DETAILED

Developed by Equity’s Science Specialist

    • day
      1

      Flight

      • Flight to Orlando, arrive and transfer to hotel, free time.
    • day
      2

      Visit to Disney’s Hollywood Studios:

      • Experience the world of movies. Attractions include movie sets, rides and thrilling stage shows.
    • day
      3

      Theme: Energy & Waves

      Spend today visiting Disney’s Epcot Centre as part of the Disney Y.E.S. Programme. Students will participate in an ‘Energy and Waves Physics Lab’ where they will learn about the properties of waves in exciting scenarios such as The Haunted Mansion®. The focus will be on sound and light waves. Additionally, the Epcot Centre offers a variety of other attractions that offer opportunities for study including:

      • Ellen’s Energy Adventure – a show about the history of the universe, evolution of the earth and energy resources
      • Living with the land – learn about ecosystems and food production
      • Circle of life – a film about conservation
      • Conservation – learn about conservation of coral reefs, sea turtles and sharks
    • day
      4

      Theme: Earth & Space

      Students will visit Kennedy Space Centre today, allowing in depth study of the history of space travel and exploration. Continue to Wonder Works, an indoor education theme park, which explores a variety of scientific themes. Opportunities for study include:

      • Natural Disasters – the science behind natural disasters
      • Physical Challenge – investigate the physics behind pressure, pulleys, bubbles and sports
      • Light and Sound – investigate the properties of light and sound
      • Space Discovery – learn about space travel and the role of an astronaut
    • day
      5

      Visit to Disney’s Magic Kingdom

      Disney themed rides, parades and shows

    • day
      6

      Visit to Universal Studios Orlando OR Islands of Adventure Park

      Universal Studios Florida® is a real working motion picture and television studio that enables guests to jump right into the action of their favourite films and TV shows 'on-set' of a variety of fully dressed backlot locations. Islands of Adventure Park offers an “unforgettable journey as the world's most cutting edge rides, shows and interactive attractions bring your favourite stories, myths, cartoons, comic books and children's tales to life.

    • day
      7

      Theme: Environment

      Travel to the Florida Everglades this morning for a Boggy Creak Airboat Ride. During the excursion there will be opportunities for studying the local ecosystems and the organisms that inhabit them, including alligators, Cypress trees and turtles. Evening flight back to the UK.

    • day
      8

      Return to UK

Subject Specific Excursions

Kennedy Space Centre

A unique chance to tour up close NASA’s launch and landing facilities. Experience interactive simulators, live shows and jaw-dropping encounters with massive rockets.

Disney® YES Programme

Disney Youth Education Series provides hands-on activities for students in applied science and environmental studies using the magic if Disney to reinforce learning.

Disney® Epcot Centre

Epcot is one of the four main Disney parks and is dedicated to technological innovation, different sections of this park are dedicated to different applications include technology and science.

WonderWorks

Students can explore the 'Wonder Zones' that are full of learning exhibits that will challenge the mind and ignite the imagination.

Universal Studios

Go behind the scenes of your favourite movies with a trip to Universal Studios. Recently opened is the Wizarding World of Harry Potter with rides and attractions.
